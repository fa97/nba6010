7a. What are (positive) Network Effects? Give an example of a product with network effects, and explain.

A positive network effect is an effect or phenomena such that the value of the product to a user increases with the total number of its users. The value of technology is a quadratic function to the number of its users.

One classic example of network effect is fax machine. It was invented in 1843 but it did not gain wide spread usage as it is useful only when there are many people using it. In 1910 newspapers started using it for commercial purposes. The more people owing a fax machine, the more valuable the fax machine is to its users. It gained wide scale adoption in 1980-1990. In this example we can also see a delay in adoption due the network effects.

7c. Read the article on Network Effects on the Web, and explain the economic problems caused by Network effects. Give an example from the article.

The internet had to be the model of free market with competition in every sector. But network effect on the web is such that, the product becomes more valuable to each user as the number of users rises and creates large information monopolies as it leads to dominance of the product. This monopoly leads to economic problems by not letting new entrants who would bring in lower prices and innovative technologies which leads to a world with fewer options. Additionally, these monopolies do not give up after their term rather do everything possible to stay in power. These over the long run stagnate the economy which once were a highly dynamic parts.

An example of this is AT&T which had near absolute dominion over the telephone from 1914-1984. It suppressed its own invention of magnetic recording for the fear that it would deter use of the telephone hence a threat to their monopoly.
